About

Hello and welcome! I'm Tahel Doron, a spirited graphic designer and multimedia artist with a heart full of art. Dive into my world where creativity knows no bounds and each design tells a story. This space reflects my journey and the philosophies that shape my work.

Artist Statement - 2024

I'm a big thinker. Big. Huge. The movie Tinkerbell (the Fairy) was one of my favorites growing up, where every Fairy had a specific magic ability, but Tinker was a thinker. Growing up, just like her, I liked to think about what I had and what to create with it, and I decided to think about thinking as inspiration. Overthinking makes me wiser. But funny enough, I struggle to make sense of how I feel. Throughout life, I've been learning from all my experiences, but the ones that made me feel the strongest are what I remember. Navigating our emotions is something every human can relate to, and we experience and view things from our personal perspective. When I create, it is the only time I don't think. I have an idea or a theme, and I just start. My process is very intuitive; I am inspired by everything I see, hear, smell, and touch. I learn more about myself through the art I create, and I hope to connect with the viewer on a deep level so that you can also connect to it from your perspective. I like to think of artists as philosophers in some way.

Art is my way of making sense of the world. I enjoy creating with every material I can find, which is also why I love graphic design. Although I still create traditionally, graphic design opened my door to a million more possibilities. I can make as many mistakes without overthinking them; I can ctrl Z them. I can create drawings with 50 colors from the comfort of my bed; I can layer paint without waiting for it to dry and use different brushes; I can use text; it is easy to share and doesn't get damaged; I can go on and on about why design is so great. But my favorite computer art is to create animations. There, I can make my drawings move, and there is where I can connect to you the most.

I often analyze several emotions in my animations rather than focus on one. I enjoy animating to the rhythm of a song and, through the visuals, convey the feelings I experience or want to explore. I use symbolism and humor to translate what I think into art; that way, you can view it without me forcing you to see it from my perspective. I love creating and viewing expressive, bold-colored, unusual art. Some artists I am inspired by are Andy Warhol, Maxime Manga, Picasso, and Barbara Kruger. Looking ahead, I am eager to delve deeper into my brain and compare concepts of innocence vs. adolescence. Thinking so much made me understand more, but I miss the times when I was curious and found everything inspiring, which I strive for today.
